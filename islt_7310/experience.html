Experience

University of Missouri – 2003 to present – Senior Support Business Specialist/Building Coordinator – Animal Sciences Research Center

In my current roll I am responsible for the Animal Sciences Research Center which is a multidisciplinary teaching and research facility. I act as the main point of contact for all problems within the facility and am tasked with ensuring that departmental equipment operates at optimal levels. I coordinate with campus maintenance and construction when equipment or the building structure needs repair. I built and maintain an Access database that I use for billing users for fees on a monthly basis. I also enter all journal entries for the facility and monitor the PeopleSoft accounting program to ensure that the building operates efficiently and at a level to support three full time employees, two part time employees, and cover department expenses while maintaining a positive balance from year to year. In addition, I operate two extra service operations which include three copiers and one large format poster printer. These tasks include making sure supplies are on hand and maintenance is performed when required. I supervise one full time research maintenance technician and at times student help. I work with the various unit leaders to monitor and adjust user fees as needed. I also address issues with reporting and accrediting agencies while making sure faculty, staff, and students are provided the resources needed to teach, learn or work while in the facility.

University of Missouri – 2002 to 2003 – Administrative Assistant – University Extension

I assisted five university extension faculty with their day to day tasks which included fielding phone calls, writing letters, preparing travel vouchers, preparing for meetings and in-service trainings, and making travel arrangements. I also assisted several state livestock extension specialists with various tasks. I helped staff with programs such as the Show-Me-Select Heifer Program, State-Fair livestock show results, and prepared the weekly commodities report which compiled data from various sources across the state. I created a website for livestock producers to get information on upcoming events and helped area hog producers implement a PDA app that aided in tracking sow productivity and litter information.

Cargill Inc. – 1997 to 2001 – Administrative Assistant – Cargill Pork Central Region

I processed orders and tracked the movement of 1.7 million head of pigs from nursery farms to contract finishing producers for the central US region. I also supported the management and central region field staff by creating weekly, monthly and yearly reports to show the profit and loss of the various contract farms within our region. I was responsible for the creation, implementation and maintenance of an automated spreadsheet used to bill producers for the pigs they received. I served as the main contact for new and existing contracts which included fielding producer questions and maintaining a database with contract begin and end dates as well as data on pigs delivered during the contract period. In addition, I administered and troubleshot issues with the office equipment including the copier, fax machine, phone and voicemail systems.
